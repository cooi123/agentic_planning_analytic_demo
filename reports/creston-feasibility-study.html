Feasibility Study Highlights

  • Financials (base case A$4,500/oz long-term gold): Post-tax NPV5% of A$1.42 billion; post-tax IRR of 24.2%; undiscounted post-tax FCF of A$2.6 billion. At spot gold (A$6,250/oz): post-tax NPV5% A$2.4bn, IRR 34.8%.
  • Production Profile: Steady-state average annual production of 120,000oz Au and 4,000t Cu at AISC of A$1,580/oz — in the lowest cost quartile of Australian gold producers.
  • Life-of-Mine Plan: 12.8Mt mined at 3.1g/t Au & 0.28% Cu for 1.27Moz gold and 36kt copper contained over 12-year mine life.
  • Ore Reserve: 12.8Mt at 3.1g/t Au & 0.28% Cu (1.27Moz Au, 36kt Cu) — a 42% increase in contained gold from the previous estimate. Creston is Australia's fifth-largest undeveloped underground gold reserve.
  • Capital Costs: Pre-production capex of A$420 million (including 10% contingency and 3% growth allowance). Post-production expansion capex of A$85 million, largely self-funded from Creston cash flows.
  • Timeline: First gold approximately 2.5 years from FID. FID targeted H1 FY27, subject to receipt of environmental approvals (WA EPA outcome targeted H2 FY26).
  • Funding: Development expected to be fully funded from Ironfield's cash reserves (A$309m at 30 September 2025) together with the A$150 million committed debt facility — without the need for equity issuance.

Overview

Ironfield Resources Limited is pleased to announce the results of the Creston Underground Project Feasibility Study (FS). The FS confirms Creston as a long-life, high-grade, lowest quartile cost underground gold-copper mine that leverages the existing Kalgara processing plant, located 25km north of the Creston orebody.

The FS was completed by a team of leading technical specialists. All technical work has been completed in accordance with the JORC Code (2012 Edition). A complete list of Competent Persons is provided in Appendix 1 of this announcement.

Mine Plan and Production Schedule

The FS Mine Plan assumes ore haulage by truck from Creston to the existing Kalgara processing plant 25km north, via the upgraded sealed haul road. Underground mining uses a long-hole open stoping method with paste fill. The Creston decline will be developed from surface via an initial 5m × 5.5m access decline, with a second access decline (ventilation/emergency egress) to follow.

Funding and Capital Structure

Ironfield expects Creston's development to be fully funded from:

  • Existing cash reserves: A$309 million at 30 September 2025, growing to approximately A$422 million at 31 December 2025, reflecting ongoing strong Kalgara operating cash flows.
  • A$150 million committed corporate debt facility (ANZ, CBA, Westpac), comprising two A$75 million revolving credit facilities, entered into September 2025.
  • Ongoing Kalgara operating cash flows during the Creston construction period (estimated A$280–360 million per annum at current gold prices).

No equity issuance is required to fund Creston's development at current gold prices. The capital structure provides significant headroom and flexibility, particularly given Kalgara's AISC guidance trending towards the lower end of A$2,200–A$2,600/oz for FY26.

Cautionary Statement: The FS Mine Plan is a production target underpinned by approximately 78% Probable Ore Reserves, 4% Indicated Mineral Resources, 12% Inferred Mineral Resources, and 6% Exploration Target (on a contained gold basis over the LOM).
