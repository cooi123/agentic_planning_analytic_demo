Guidance Assumptions and Key Points

Production Profile

FY26 production is expected to be slightly weighted towards H1, reflecting the higher-grade underground ore blend in the first half of the year and the planned November mill shutdown in Q2 FY26. Open pit production from West Pit Stage 4 remains the primary feed source, supplemented by Lower Kalgara Underground ore.

AISC

AISC guidance of A$2,200–A$2,600/oz reflects full-year costs at Kalgara, net of by-product copper credits. AISC is expected to be weighted towards the first half of FY26, with Q1 and Q2 benefiting from higher underground ore blend. Processing costs will be elevated in November 2025 due to the scheduled mill maintenance shutdown.

Sustaining Capital

Sustaining capital of A$40–50 million covers planned mill maintenance, underground development sustaining, village and site infrastructure maintenance, and rehabilitation provisions. The phased open pit fleet rebuild program (8 trucks over FY26) is included in sustaining capital.

Growth Capital

Total growth capital guidance of A$155–185 million includes: Kalgara growth (A$80–95m: TSF6 Stage 2 lift, North Pit Stage 2 pre-stripping, LKU growth development); Creston (A$58–72m: Feasibility Study completion, early works, decline tunnelling); and resource development (A$18–22m: 220,000m drill program, 10 rigs).

FY26 Strategic Priorities

1. Kalgara Operational Excellence

Continued focus on open pit productivity improvement in West Pit Stage 4 and North Pit Stage 2 waste stripping. Fleet renewal (8 truck rebuilds) and RC grade control roll-out are the key operational enablers for production trending towards the upper end of guidance.

2. Creston Feasibility Study and FID Preparation

Completion and release of the Creston Feasibility Study (October 2025), followed by FID preparation including finalisation of the A$150 million debt facility and contractor selection. Early works investment will continue throughout FY26 to advance critical-path activities ahead of FID (targeted H1 FY27).

3. Resource Growth — 220,000 Metre Drill Program

The largest resource development drill program in Demo Company A's history is underway at Kalgara, with 10 active rigs targeting Stage 3/4 Extension (open pit mine life extension beyond FY27), Lower Kalgara Underground maiden Resource Estimate (Q3 FY26), and near-mine satellite deposits within trucking distance of the Kalgara processing plant.

4. Environmental Approvals — Creston

Active engagement with the WA EPA and Commonwealth EPBC Act assessment processes. Both approvals are targeted in H2 FY26 and are prerequisites for the Creston FID. Demo Company A has engaged specialist environmental consultants to support the process.
