Principal Activities

The Group's principal activities during the period consisted of mining, production and sale of gold and copper at the Kalgara Gold-Copper Mine, development of the Creston Underground Project, and the exploration and evaluation of mineral tenements in Western Australia.

Demo Company A owns 100% of the Kalgara Gold-Copper Mine, located in the Murchison Province of Western Australia, approximately 650km north of Perth. The mine is a fly-in fly-out operation with both open pit and underground mining, an established workforce and significant infrastructure. Gold and copper are produced by a 10Mtpa processing plant, which produces gold doré and a copper-gold concentrate.

During H1 FY26, the Kalgara Mine produced 83,417 ounces of gold and 3,037 tonnes of copper at an All-In Sustaining Cost of A$2,341/oz. A key driver of the period's performance was consistently high recovery rates of approximately 87.2% for gold and 78.8% for copper. Total material mined increased by 28% relative to the previous six-month period driven by focus on increasing open pit productivities and opening up North Pit Stage 2 working areas.

One lost time injury occurred during the period, resulting in a 12-month moving average LTIFR of 0.3. The TRIFR improved to 4.4 at 31 December 2025.

Mining

Open pit ore mined during H1 FY26 was from West Pit Stage 4 and North Pit Stage 1, totalling 3.87Mt at 0.61g/t Au and 0.08% Cu. Dump leach ore mined was 0.64Mt. Total material mined was 11.42Mt including approximately 6.89Mt of waste, a 28% increase on the previous six-month period. North Pit Stage 2 waste stripping commenced during the period, with 3.4Mt of waste mined cumulatively to December 2025; this cutback is expected to provide baseload ore feed from FY28.

Lower Kalgara Underground (LKU) ore mined was predominantly from C-Lode and the Eastern Corridor, totalling 0.47Mt at 1.78g/t Au and 0.60% Cu. Underground development metres totalled 2,628m for the period, including 1,480m of growth capital development. Exceptional LKU drilling results, including 48.6m @ 6.2g/t Au from 412m, indicate ongoing system growth and support a maiden Mineral Resource Estimate targeted in the March 2026 quarter.

Processing

H1 FY26 delivered processed tonnes of 4.82Mt with average head grade of 0.62g/t Au and 0.09% Cu. Recoveries of approximately 87.2% for gold and 78.8% for copper were maintained throughout the period. Gold production of 83,417oz represented a strong H1 performance and positions Demo Company A to trend towards the upper end of FY26 guidance of 130,000–155,000oz Au.

Creston Underground Project — Development Review

The Creston Underground Project is Demo Company A's high-grade gold-copper development project located 25km south of the Kalgara processing plant. The project represents the next major growth driver for Demo Company A and is expected to leverage existing Kalgara processing infrastructure.

Feasibility Study

The Creston Feasibility Study (FS) was completed and announced on 3 October 2025, confirming the pathway to a world-class, long-life, lowest quartile cost Australian gold-copper mine. Key highlights of the FS:

  • Undiscounted free cash flow of A$3.8 billion pre-tax and A$2.6 billion post-tax at base case (A$4,500/oz long-term gold)
  • NPV5% of A$2.0 billion pre-tax and A$1.42 billion post-tax; increases to A$3.4bn pre-tax at spot gold (A$6,250/oz)
  • Post-tax IRR of 24.2% at A$4,500/oz gold (34.8% at A$6,250/oz)
  • Life-of-Mine Plan: 12.8Mt mined at 3.1g/t Au & 0.28% Cu for 1.27Moz gold and 36kt copper
  • Steady-state annual production of 120,000oz Au at AISC of A$1,580/oz
  • Pre-production capex of A$420 million (including 10% contingency)
  • Initial mine life of 12 years; Updated Ore Reserve of 12.8Mt at 3.1g/t Au & 0.28% Cu (1.27Moz Au, 36kt Cu)

Early Works & Permitting

Decline access tunnelling reached 712m cumulative by 31 December 2025. Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) submitted to the WA EPA in September 2025; EPA approval is targeted in H2 FY26 and is a prerequisite for the Final Investment Decision (FID), which is targeted in H1 FY27. A total of A$38.2 million was invested in Creston during H1 FY26 (FS completion and early works).

Financial Performance

The Group recorded a net profit after tax of A$155.5 million for H1 FY26 (31 December 2025), compared to A$28.4 million in H1 FY25. The prior period included only approximately one month of Kalgara ownership, following completion of the acquisition on 6 December 2024.

Revenue for H1 FY26 was A$460.8 million, primarily comprising gold revenue of A$440.4 million and copper revenue of A$20.4 million. Gold and copper sales benefited from full upside exposure to strong commodity prices. Sales comprised 76,800 ounces of gold at an average realised price of A$5,742/oz, and 3,277 tonnes of copper at an average realised copper price of A$13,362/t.

Cost of sales was A$228.6 million, comprising site production costs of A$152.4 million, employee benefits of A$28.2 million, royalties of A$16.8 million, selling costs of A$6.4 million, changes in inventories of A$(8.6) million, and depreciation and amortisation of A$33.4 million. Processing costs were higher in the December quarter than the September quarter, due primarily to planned mill maintenance costs and increased reagent usage from processing higher-clay Stage 4 ore.

Balance Sheet

Total assets increased by A$268.5 million to A$1,380.5 million at 31 December 2025 (30 June 2025: A$1,112.0 million), primarily reflecting the cash build from Kalgara operations and capital expenditure on Kalgara and Creston. Total liabilities increased by A$114.6 million to A$545.0 million, including a A$22.4 million contract liability recognised for a copper-gold concentrate shipment where cash was received in December 2025 but loading completed in January 2026.

Cash Flow

Group cash flow from operations for H1 FY26 was A$318.2 million, with a cash build of A$193.2 million delivering a closing cash balance at 31 December 2025 of A$421.8 million (30 June 2025: A$228.6 million), debt free. The Group invested A$119.1 million in capital expenditure and resource development, comprising A$22.6 million sustaining capital, A$44.2 million Kalgara growth capital, A$38.2 million Creston early works, and A$16.2 million resource development. Financing outflows of A$5.9 million related primarily to lease principal repayments.
